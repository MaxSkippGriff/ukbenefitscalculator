Methodology

This calculator uses deterministic formulas based on published UK rates for {{ tax_year }}. It estimates PAYE income tax, employee National Insurance, student loan repayments and pension deductions for planning and salary comparison. It is not payroll software and it is not financial advice.

What we apply

  • Region-specific income tax bands (rUK and Scotland)
  • Employee NI Category A assumptions (unless specified otherwise)
  • Student loan plan thresholds and repayment rates
  • Pension contribution effects including salary sacrifice toggle
  • Tax code allowance parsing with taper effects where applicable

Calculation sequence (simplified)

  1. Start with gross annual salary and convert to a clean annual basis for calculation.
  2. Apply the selected region’s income tax bands and rates, using tax-code allowances where relevant.
  3. Calculate employee National Insurance using the selected NI category assumptions.
  4. Apply student loan repayment thresholds and rates for the selected plan (if any).
  5. Apply pension deductions based on contribution percentage and salary sacrifice setting.
  6. Derive annual, monthly, weekly and daily take-home outputs for planning use.

This sequence is designed to produce a practical planning estimate. Employer payroll systems may differ in presentation and timing.

How to improve estimate quality

  • Use the correct UK nation (Scotland uses different income tax bands).
  • Select the correct student loan plan; using the wrong plan can materially change monthly net pay.
  • Match your pension setting and salary sacrifice option to your real employment setup.
  • Check your tax code from a recent payslip if you are using this for budgeting or offer comparison.
  • For side-by-side comparisons, keep all assumptions constant and change only salary or region.

Known limitations

  • Does not model every payroll edge case or employer-specific setup
  • Does not include benefits-in-kind and all one-off deductions
  • Multi-employment and cumulative code situations may vary from estimate

Reference sources and update process

We review published HMRC and UK government guidance when tax-year assumptions change and update calculator inputs accordingly. We also review related guide content so page explanations stay aligned with the active calculation logic.

  • GOV.UK: Income Tax rates and bands
  • GOV.UK: National Insurance rates and categories
  • GOV.UK: Student loan repayment thresholds and rates
